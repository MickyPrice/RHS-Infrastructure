Motherboard

A motherboard is a large printed circuit board responsible for the transport and housing of information as it passes to the central processing unit (CPU), it uses copper or gold-alloy wires plated onto a glass epoxy board enabling multiple connections to be made in a small space, the motherboard is directly connected to the RAM drives and CPU with slots made in it accordingly, and is connected by external wires to the other components.
